Switchpoint Rental Assistance Programs Success Stories

Below are four client success stories. Each of these clients received a long-term housing assistance grant. These stories are true; however, names have been replaced with initials or identifiers to protect client privacy.

Story 1

P & D are a married couple in their 60s who receive Social Security benefits. They became homeless after an eviction that occurred when they helped a family member going through a crisis. At that time, they entered the Switchpoint Shelter and qualified for long-term housing assistance through a grant administered by the Switchpoint Housing Team.

The couple has experienced many hardships, including both recently battling major medical issues. Regardless of everything they have gone through, they have remained optimistic about their future and credit the grant and the support provided by the Shelter Team and their Switchpoint Housing Case Manager for the stability they now have in their lives. At Switchpoint, we credit their willingness to make changes and their commitment to each other and to building a stable life together.

    Some of the activities that highlight this couple’s resilience and positive outlook include:
    • This past Thanksgiving, P & D volunteered at the local St. George Community Thanksgiving Dinner, wishing to give back to those less fortunate.
    • D enjoys participating in her community through her church.
    • They both speak with great optimism about their future beyond the grant.
    • While receiving rental assistance through the grant, the couple was able to build savings and an emergency fund so they can remain financially self-sufficient no matter what life brings.

The Switchpoint team is in awe of this couple and their commitment to always moving forward with positivity.

Story 2

This family of four had been through challenging times for several years. Dad was homeless on and off for more than two years, most recently ending in residential treatment for alcohol use. Mom and the two children had been living in what is formally known as a place not meant for habitation — in this case, a broken-down building with no electricity or running water.

With the support of the Switchpoint Shelter and Housing Teams through a long-term housing assistance grant, this family was able to come together and move beyond simply maintaining to truly thriving.

With the guidance of their Housing Case Manager, the family reunited in their own safe and permanent rental home. With parenting education support, the parents were able to work together to co-parent their children with positivity and love while building financial stability for their family. Mom and Dad dedicated themselves to rebuilding a positive, loving family environment and pursuing their goals with determination.

    Some of the many accomplishments the family achieved include:
    • Dad worked with DWS to secure and maintain eligible part-time employment.
    • Dad continued working on his SSDI application process.
    • Dad maintained sobriety and consistently met the requirements of his probation.
    • Mom started employment at $9 per hour and later secured better employment at $16 per hour with Digby’s. She is extremely grateful for the benefits that others may take for granted, including paid time off, healthcare benefits, and a 401K plan. Mom shared emotionally that she never imagined having a job that provides so much support and says she truly loves her work and employer.
    • While receiving rental assistance through the housing grant, Mom and Dad paid off debts, including long-standing court fines and fees.
    • Mom successfully regained her Driver’s License.
    • The family was able to replace their unreliable vehicle with a more dependable car.
    • The now 18-year-old son, who has ongoing health challenges and receives SSDI, is working with Vocational Rehabilitation to potentially obtain employment.
    • Their 15-year-old daughter is on track to graduate high school and consistently attends therapy appointments.

This family has been very vocal in their appreciation of the Switchpoint team. In truth, we are equally appreciative of the opportunity to support this amazing family. They have done all the hard work themselves, and we are proud to have been part of their journey.

Story 3

S had been homeless during her addiction, living in survival mode and separated from her beloved child. She began her slow and steady journey toward healing, self-reliance, and happiness when she entered the Switchpoint Shelter. Her progress continued with the support of long-term housing assistance and a dedicated Switchpoint Housing Case Manager.

S’s focus has always been not only reuniting with her now-teenage child but also rebuilding a trusting and close relationship. She has succeeded.

  • S fully committed to her recovery and continues to draw strength and support from her NA community each day.
  • She obtained employment with a local grocery store and quickly advanced through promotions and pay raises, demonstrating her dedication and work ethic.
  • Her child, C, was able to return to live with S in the permanent home she worked so hard to obtain and maintain.
  • S spends her spare time nurturing her relationship with C and is dedicated to building a lasting bond that breaks the cycle of addiction and homelessness.
  • S credits the parenting and communication skills she learned through Switchpoint for strengthening their relationship.
    • They enjoy cooking and eating meals together.
    • They love hiking and camping together.
    • C attends Utah Arts Academy and is thriving in the creative and academic environment.
    • S recently taught C to drive — something neither of them could have imagined a year ago.
    • They recently baked banana bread together and delivered it as thank-you gifts to the individuals and organizations that supported their journey.

S recently informed her Switchpoint Housing Case Manager that she no longer needs housing grant support. She shared that she and C are now financially stable and able to maintain their home independently. She specifically asked that the grant be used to help another family beginning their own journey toward stability. It has been a privilege to witness their strength, love, and success.

Story 4

V is a survivor of sex trafficking and domestic violence. She survived years of homelessness, living in the underground tunnels beneath Las Vegas alongside hundreds of other homeless individuals — sometimes referred to as the “Las Vegas Mole People.”

V is a warrior. She fights every day for herself, her son, her family, and for other survivors of sex trafficking. Her healing journey began when she made the courageous decision to leave Las Vegas, her traffickers, and her abusers behind. She returned home to St. George, where she began receiving support from the local domestic violence shelter.

By the time she was selected for a long-term housing grant through Switchpoint, V had already begun replacing survival coping mechanisms with healthy alternatives. She was working full-time as a food server and had begun the court process to reunite with her teenage son.

With stable housing obtained through the grant, V continued working every day to rebuild her life and create a future filled with hope.

  • She began addressing past legal challenges, paying court fines, and working to rebuild family relationships.
  • She successfully completed all legal requirements necessary to welcome her son back into her home.
  • Once reunited with her son, she found employment that allowed her to spend evenings and weekends with him.
  • She actively participates in a recovery group for survivors of sex trafficking.
    • She and her 17-year-old son now share a close and trusting relationship.
    • Her son is a proud member of his high school marching band and participates in the school’s ROTC program.
    • V enjoys babysitting her adult daughter’s two infants and is deeply grateful for the trust restored in their relationship.
    • Beyond her own healing, V now volunteers to support other survivors of sex trafficking as they begin their own journeys toward recovery.

V is a warrior and an inspiration to the Switchpoint team and to everyone who has the privilege of knowing her.
